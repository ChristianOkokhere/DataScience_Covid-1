Coronavirus and Intersectionality: New York City

A deep dive into how the Coronavirus pandemic may be exposing citywide and possibly countrywide healthcare inequalities

A project created by: Tyrell Harris and Christian Okokhere


Introduction:

This project was conceptualized just after half an hour of reading various reports about minority populations making up significantly large portions of Coronavirus cases and deaths in cities across the country despite these populations being just that: minorities. We decided to look at one of the biggest hotspots for the pandemic in the United States, New York City (which has a whopping 230,000 cases and 23,000 deaths), and find out just how disproportionatly minorities are being affected by Covid-19 compared to other groups. But we also wanted to take it a bit further than that and look at what other variables may have a correlation with high or low case and death numbers, specifically income and what its role may be in this story. What we now have is lots of data to share that gives us a clearer picture of how income and race, and the connections between them, play an unfortunatly large role in who is currently getting and dying from Coronavirus.

Racial Demographic of NYC:

To better understand the visualizations we have created below, some context regarding what type of people live in New York City is provided. Keep in mind that because of the dynamic nature of an ongoing pandemic, there is only so much data regarding the race and ethnicity of cases available. Because of this we will only be looking at data on groups identifying as White, Black, Asian/Pacific Islander, or of Hispanic origin. Native American, Multi-Racial, and Other groups are not included in any of our calculations.
