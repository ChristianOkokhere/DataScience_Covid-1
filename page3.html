Income Inequality in NYC

NYC Demographic Coronavirus and Race

We gathered data on the richest and poorest neigborhoods of each NYC Borough to see what type of people are living in certain financial conditions. The goal was to give credence to the idea that minority communities are more susceptible to Coronavirus partly because they are financially worse off than White communties. For these scatter plots, each row of horizontally aligned dots represents a neighborhood of a certain income. The individual dots on these rows represent different races and the percentage of the population they make up (Mouse over the graphs for more info). These neigborhoods in total only account for around 2.4 million people or 27% of our modified NYC population but these charts still make it very clear which groups are more well off than others.

(Manhattan) Blue = Black, Red = White, Green = Hispanic, Yellow = Asian

As you can see, one of the richest neighborhoods in Manhattan such as Flatiron District, has a demographic of 69% White, 19% Asian, 7% Hispanic, and 3% Black. While one of the poorest neighborhoods, Lower East-Side, is 2.7% White, 11.5% Black, 38.4% Asian, and 47.4% Hispanic. These sort of trends pop-up for each of the Boroughs' scatter plots, with white people making up most of the rich communities and minorities making up most of the poor communities..

All of this heavily suggests a sort of self-perpetuating cycle. Since poorer communities are predominantly Hispanic or Black, Covid-19 infects and kills minorities moreso than it does White people. But it is also most likely that people with less money, regardless of race, are the ones taking the biggest hits from this pandemic. And with upward mobility being nothing more than a very unlikely dream, minorities will continue to make up the majority of these poor communities and be without the resources required to seek out quality health care.
